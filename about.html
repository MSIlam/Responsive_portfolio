Why web development?

As a researcher, I've cultivated an innate ability for problem-solving, idea development, and collaborative growth. Additionally, I've always been fascinated by the inner workings of technology, particularly how it transforms data to impact global reach. My venture into coding commenced with my initial encounter with R, where I explored its capabilities in analyzing extensive datasets. This experience ignited a deep curiosity within me, propelling me to immerse myself in web development at a coding school, a decision I've embraced wholeheartedly ever since. I'm continuously advancing in my technical skills through consistent practice and unwavering determination. My ultimate goal is to create innovative tools that drive both scientific progress and societal advancement. In a forward-thinking company, I aim to leverage my diverse skill set.

Past experiences

During my undergraduate years, my love for the natural world grew as I delved deep into the Bangladeshi forests to collect data on wildlife behavior while studying zoology. This immersive experience ignited a deep appreciation for environmental sustainability within me, a sentiment that only strengthened during my master's studies in Ecology in Germany. Motivated by a passion for creating positive change, I set out on a path in soil biogeochemistry, pursuing a PhD dedicated to sustainable soil management aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions from agricultural areas. In this era of pressing climate concerns, I strongly advocate for the widespread sharing of knowledge as essential for progress toward our shared objectives. My journey has been shaped by a passion for data-driven decision-making, analyzing diverse datasets ranging from small-scale lab experiments to large spatial time-series data, totaling between 10000-15000 data points. My analytical prowess is complemented by a track record of three publications in scientific journals, two articles in regional magazines, and active participation in two prestigious international conferences. Additionally, I've had the privilege of supervising four graduate students and fostering collaborative relationships with two research groups and six farms.
I am now eager to transition into an industry role where I can integrate my diverse skill set and contribute to impactful projects in sustainability and technology.
